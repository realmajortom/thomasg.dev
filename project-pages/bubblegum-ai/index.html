EHDI Data Management in Three Steps:

Screening is the first step in managing a newborn hearing program. HiTrack's user-friendly interface helps medical providers import demographic data and screening results from screening equipment.

HiTrack's intelligent tracking system indicates babies in need of follow-up services and recommends personalized "next-steps" in a baby's treatment plan. In addition, users can easily generate specialized lists of patients with upcoming or overdue appointments.

Our program also assists in bridging communication between local health care providers, hospitals, and state and regional agencies. Based on children's treatment progress and screening program status, HiTrack automatically generates customizable letters and reports to share with these parties. This synchronicity of information helps improve the personalization of medical care and increases patient participation in treatment programs.

Features

Hearing Screening Protocol Engine™

HiTrack's Hearing Screening Protocol Engine™ is a rule-based system that governs how infants are tracked. Based on the screening protocol selected by the Screening Program Coordinator, the software automatically suggests the "next step" needed for each baby being tracked through the screening and follow-up process. Users can select a protocol to best suit their facility's data tracking needs:

  • Two-Stage Automated Pass/Manual Non-Pass
  • Two-Stage Fully Automated
  • Two-Stage Manual
  • Two-Stage Inpatient Automated
  • Two-Stage Dual Equipment Auto-Pass Only
  • Two-Stage Dual Equipment Automated

Preferences/Settings

  • Access rights can be administrated by creating custom User Groups
  • Facilities and their specific equipment configurations can be entered and modified
  • Physicians, screeners and audiologists can be entered and modified
  • Alternate IDs for medical personnel can be entered to help facilitate linking with third-party screening equipment software
  • Nursery, Early Intervention, and other demographic pick lists can be customized
  • Facilities can determine the default recipient of both standard and custom letters
  • Required fields can be defined for manual data-entry
  • Facility Groups can be defined for reporting and list filtering

Facility Baby List

  • Based on a facility's protocol setting, infants are categorized automatically based on their real-time needs
  • Infants in need of inpatient or outpatient screening can be easily identified
  • Recommendation Actions are provided to help medical providers and patients arrange future appointments
  • Lists can be sorted, filtered, and searched based on a large variety of criteria
  • The birth facility and birth date filter allow users to work with subsets of data
  • Lists of babies who have been transferred in or out are provided by default
  • Infants who pass screening with risk indicators entered are automatically placed in a Risk Monitoring list
  • CDC Milestone Tracking utilizing filters for 'Met', 'Due Soon', 'Overdue' and 'Late'

Import & Merge Data

HiTrack's advanced rule-based merge protocols are optimized to ensure data integrity while providing edge-case flexibility. Data from external sources are merged in batches allowing error-free records to flow into the central database while questionable records are set aside for correction at the user's convenience.

  • Configure automatic, time-based uploads
  • Manually resolve questionable or abnormal records
  • Manually resolve "Unknown Codes" from screening equipment
  • Support for most popular screening equipment
  • Import and merge patient demographic data from hospital data systems
  • Import and merge lists of Physicians, Audiologists and Screeners from hospital data systems.
  • Review Merge History
