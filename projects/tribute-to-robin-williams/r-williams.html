Robin Williams

"I love kids, but they are a tough audience."

I've written and rewritten my "tribute" Robin Williams for days now. It feels borderline sacrilegous to cram the expanse of his accomplishments and the depth of his legacy into a few paragrphs on a "learning-to-code" project. Instead of slathering a wall of generic biography-vomit, I'll share an experience of my own.

One of my favorite movies growing up was Hook, with Robin Williams as Peter Pan. In the first part of the film, adult Peter winds up in Neverland. However, he has forgotton who he is and must take lessons from the Lost Boys on how to fight, fly, and crow. It's the only way he can rescue his children from Captain Hook.

At the film's turning point, Peter remembers who his--he finds his Happy Thought. This awakening is ushered in with a hilarious food fight; Thud Butt flicks rainbow frosting and Pan, Latchboy chucks a fistful at Pockets and Donut, and mayhem is unleashed. The fight ends abruptly when a coconut soars towards Peter, who insinctively deflects it with his sword. Pan is back.

Only Robin Williams could have pulled that off. The boyish joy he eminates in the film is tangible, contagious. As an adult, the scene still brings me the excitment I felt as a 6-year-old boy. I wish Robin could hear the laughs he brought to my home, and I'm sure many others feel the same.

Bangarang, Robin!
